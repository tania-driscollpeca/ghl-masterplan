Section B: Critical Functions You Probably Forgot to Audit

These are the traps that destroy migrations. They're not obvious, but each one can cause lead loss, operational errors, or team rejection of the system.

01
Ring Timeout & Voicemail Settings

FUB configures rings before voicemail per user. In GHL it's in Phone Settings per number. Without configuring, calls will route incorrectly.

02
Lead Source Attribution

FUB saves the source automatically. In GHL it must be captured via UTM params or a hidden form field. Without this, you lose attribution from Showcase, website, and Facebook ads.

03
Round Robin Assignment Logic

FUB has native round robin. GHL also has it but via Workflow with conditionals. If not rebuilt identically, leads go to the wrong agent from Day 1.

04
Duplicate Lead Handling

FUB merges duplicates with defined logic. GHL creates duplicates if there's no active deduplication workflow. Can contaminate the entire database.

05
Do Not Contact / Opt-Out (CASL)

FUB has a DNC field. GHL has opt-out per channel. If not mapped before migrating, you'll send messages to contacts who requested no contact. Legal risk under CASL in Canada.

06
Email Deliverability — SPF/DKIM/DMARC

GHL requires you to configure SPF/DKIM for your domain. Without this, all nurture emails go to spam from Day 1. Agents assume GHL doesn't work.

07
Timezone Settings per User and Workflow

Workflows configured in UTC send SMS at 3am Ontario time. Silent error with devastating consequences for first impressions.

08
Task Reminders on Mobile

Push notifications in Lead Connector require device-specific configuration. Without this, agents don't receive task alerts.

09
Pipeline Visibility by Role

Without configured permissions, agents see other agents' leads. Creates confusion and internal conflicts from Day 1.

10
Action Plan Pausing Logic

In FUB you can pause an Action Plan without deleting it. In GHL the flow is different. Without training, agents don't know how to stop an active sequence.

11
Google Calendar Sync

Without sync, agents see appointments in GHL but not in Google Calendar. Double-bookings in the first week. Irritated clients.

12
Historical Data Accessibility

FUB history (notes, calls, emails) doesn't live in GHL. You need a plan: export CSV and keep FUB in read-only for 90 days.

13
Bulk Stage Change Limitations

In FUB you can change the stage of multiple contacts at once. In GHL Bulk Actions exist but with limitations. Verify before assuming equivalence.

14
Email Open/Click Tracking Scope

GHL only tracks opens on emails sent via Conversations or Workflows, not on direct emails sent from a connected Gmail account.

15
Showcase Integration Verification

If Showcase sends leads to FUB via Zapier and the Zap disconnects during migration, Showcase leads disappear without anyone noticing for days.

Section C: Ideal GHL Architecture for Driscoll-Peca

Golden rule: A lead enters ONE pipeline based on their first declared intent. If intent changes, a new opportunity is created in the correct pipeline. The contact doesn't move — only the opportunity does.

Pipelines

Pipeline: Buyer

  • Sources: VIP List, Buyer Guide, Upsizing, Open House, Coming Soon, Buyers
  • Stages: New Lead → A Hot (1-3 mo) → B Warm (3-6 mo) → C Cold (6+) → Nurture → Active Client → Pending → Closed
  • On entry: Tag 'intent:buyer', assign via round robin, SMS welcome + email nurture Day 1
  • On Active Client: auto-task "Send MLS Search" within 24h
  • On Pending: Tag 'status:pending', launch closing checklist automation

Pipeline: Seller

  • Sources: Home Valuation, Seller Guide, Nosey Neighbor
  • Stages: New Lead → A Hot → B Warm → C Cold → Nurture → Active Client → Pending → Closed
  • On entry: Tag 'intent:seller', send CMA request email, assign to listing agent
  • On Active Client: task "Coordinate listing appointment"
  • On Pending: launch transaction coordination checklist

Pipeline: General

  • For non-buyer/seller leads: blog, market reports, Rogue Community, contact forms
  • Purpose: long-term nurture until they express buying/selling intent
  • Key automation: if they complete a buyer/seller form → move to correct pipeline automatically
  • Simplified stages: New → Engaged → Nurture → Converted

Section H: Honest Recommendations

Where GHL Clearly Wins

  • Omnichannel Conversations: SMS, email, chat, FB, Instagram in one inbox. FUB is fundamentally email-first.
  • Workflow Builder: enormously more powerful. Complex conditional logic that FUB simply cannot do.
  • Native Landing Pages and Funnels. FUB doesn't have this. Eliminates an external tool directly.
  • Native Chatbot / AI Agent with conditional routing by page source.
  • Full SMS and Voice: A2P, VM drop, call recording all within the same system.
  • Cost at scale: as the team grows, GHL is significantly cheaper than FUB + satellite tools.
  • Sub-accounts: perfect architecture for the Rogue Community program and future expansion.
  • Campaign reporting: connects ad spend to pipeline conversions. FUB doesn't do this natively.

Where FUB Still Wins

  • Smart Lists as a daily work tool: FUB does them better. Simple, visual, always updated.
  • Agent UI speed: FUB designed for real estate. GHL designed for marketers. More daily operational friction.
  • Learning curve: FUB in days. GHL in weeks. Without structured training, rejection is inevitable.
  • Lead Profile view: FUB shows the most relevant lead info more clearly on the initial view.
  • Pre-built integrations with real estate portals.
  • Real estate-focused support. GHL Support is known for being inconsistent in response speed.

Where to Adapt Instead of Copy

  • Smart Lists: DON'T replicate exactly. Use Custom Views + proactive workflows that bring leads to the agent's attention.
  • Action Plans: DON'T copy FUB's format. Leverage conditionals, behavior-based branching, and intelligent delays.
  • Pipeline Stages: DON'T copy FUB stages without reviewing them. Opportunity to redesign the actual sales process.
  • Email threads: DON'T try to replicate FUB's email behavior. Omnichannel Conversations is objectively better.
  • Notifications: GHL can do intelligent routing: if not responded in 5 min → notify team lead. Use this.
  • Reporting: Don't ask for the same FUB reports. Design new ones that leverage what GHL can measure that FUB couldn't.

The most honest advice:

GHL migrations fail in real estate teams not for technical reasons. They fail because the system feels slower for the agent's daily work. If you don't solve that with deep training and Custom Views equivalent to Smart Lists, agents go back to FUB or direct email.

The most important investment in this migration is not the configuration: it's the training. Designate one person as the internal GHL Champion who resolves problems in real time during the first 30 days. Without this resource, adoption drops below 50% even if everything is technically perfect.

About timing:

Don't migrate during peak season. If peak is spring/summer, start in October-November to be stable in January. Migrating with 10+ active transactions simultaneously is a recipe for chaos and permanent system rejection.

About the course investment:

The recorded internal course is the highest-ROI asset of this migration. An agent who watches the 20-minute Custom Views module before touching the system is worth 10 times more than one who arrives on Day 1 unprepared. Record it before go-live, without exception.

FAQ: Detailed Questions by Profile

Exact operational answers for the real questions each profile will have during and after the migration.

Agents
›Where are my Smart Lists? I don't see anything similar when I open GHL.

FUB Smart Lists don't exist as a native concept in GHL, and that's the most important mental adjustment you need to make. Your equivalent tool is called Custom Views inside Contacts and inside each Pipeline.

What we built before you arrived: the views equivalent to your most-used Smart Lists, saved and named the same: HOT TODAY, NEEDS FOLLOW-UP, NEW THIS WEEK, MY LEADS, ACTIVE CLIENTS, PENDING TRANSACTIONS.

How to access them: left menu → Contacts → filter icon top right → saved views. To work by pipeline: Opportunities → choose the pipeline → filter by Stage → save view.

The key difference: GHL is more proactive. Instead of you going to find who needs contact, the system creates automatic tasks telling you who to call today.

›How do I move a lead to a different stage? Is there anything I need to do besides drag it?

Yes, there are steps you shouldn't skip. How to move: Opportunities → drag the card to the new stage, OR open the lead → click the current stage to change it.

What you MUST do in addition:

1. Add a brief note explaining why it changed. Example: "Moved to A Hot because they confirmed pre-approval and 60-day timeline." Essential for the team lead to understand context.

2. Check if an automatic task was created. When you move to Active Client, a task is created automatically. Find it in the Tasks tab.

3. DON'T move leads to Closed Lost without adding a reason in the notes. It's strategic information for the team.

Automations that fire on stage change: New Lead → Engaged cancels the welcome sequence if they already replied. Any stage → Pending notifies the team lead and starts the transaction checklist. Any stage → Closed Won starts the referral sequence and Google review request.

›How do I reply to a lead who texted me? Is it different from replying to an email?

No, that's one of the best parts of GHL: everything is in the same place. Go to Conversations in the left menu. Unified inbox: SMS, email, Facebook, Instagram, website chat, all together. Each conversation shows the channel with an icon.

To reply via SMS: open the conversation → SMS channel is already selected → type and send. If you want to change the reply channel, there's a channel selector before the text field.

Important details: if the lead has an active automatic nurture sequence and you reply manually, GHL pauses the sequence to avoid sending duplicate messages. That behavior is called "smart stop" and is configured in the workflows. Always reply from Conversations, not from your connected Gmail, so everything stays recorded in the system.

›Can I call directly from GHL? How does Voicemail Drop work?

Yes, and it's one of the most powerful features. From iPad or desktop: go to the contact → phone button → click to call. The call goes out from the team number assigned to you. It records automatically.

From mobile: use the Lead Connector app.

VM Drop: a pre-recorded message you deposit when a lead's phone goes to voicemail, without it ringing even once. The lead sees they have a voicemail but you didn't have to speak. How to use it: you detect it's going to voicemail → tap "Drop Voicemail" → select the recording → hang up. The VM deposits itself.

We have two configured: VM Warm for leads with history (more personal), VM Cold for first contact (introduces the team).

›How do I add a call note? How much detail should I include?

Go to the contact → Notes tab → "+ Add Note". You can also add a note from Conversations directly on that person's thread.

Standardized format for the team:

[DATE] Call - [approx duration]
Result: answered / no answer / left VM
Summary: what they said, what they asked, what you promised
Next step: what you'll do and when
Alert: something important the team should know

›What's the correct anatomy of a workflow? What do I need to understand first?

A workflow has 4 components. Master them in this order:

1. TRIGGER: what event initiates it. Form submitted, Contact tag added, Pipeline stage changed, No activity in X days, Date/Time, Inbound message received.

MOST COMMON ERROR: trigger too broad. "Contact Created" without filters fires the workflow on all imported contacts too.

2. FILTERS / CONDITIONS: additional conditions so it only runs when appropriate. Example: Form Submitted + Source = "open-house-form" only.

3. ACTIONS: Send Email/SMS, Add/Remove Tag, Add to Pipeline, Move Stage, Assign Contact, Create Task, Wait (delay), If/Else, Add Note, Webhook.

4. GOALS / EXIT CONDITIONS: what event causes the contact to exit the workflow before completing it. Example: if the lead replies, they exit the automatic sequence.

Best practice: draw the workflow on paper before building it in GHL. Include all possible paths. Building without that map creates workflows with holes that drop leads.

›How do I configure SPF/DKIM so emails don't go to spam?

The most critical technical step before go-live.

1. Settings → Email Services → Email Domain → Add Domain → driscollpeca.com

2. GHL generates DNS records: TXT for SPF, two CNAMEs for DKIM.

3. Go to your domain's DNS panel. AgentFire probably manages it and can add the records.

4. Add exactly the records GHL shows. Copy and paste, don't type manually.

5. Propagation: 24-48 hours. Verify beforehand at mxtoolbox.com

6. GHL → Settings → Email Domain → "Verify" → must show green on all fields.

Additional DMARC: TXT record _dmarc.driscollpeca.com → value: v=DMARC1; p=none; rua=mailto:info@driscollpeca.com

Before any bulk campaign: send 5 test emails to Gmail, Outlook, and Apple Mail. Verify they land in inbox, not spam. Verify the "From" shows the agent's name. Verify the Unsubscribe button works (CASL requirement).

›How do I import contacts from FUB without triggering automations on all of them?

This is the number one risk of the migration. If not done correctly, you send thousands of messages to historical contacts.

STEP 1: Export from FUB as CSV. Separate: active leads vs historical/closed leads.

STEP 2: Clean CSV before importing. Remove duplicates. Identify opt-outs. Verify phone format (+1XXXXXXXXXX).

CRITICAL STEP 3: DEACTIVATE all workflows with trigger "Contact Created" or "Tag Added" before importing. Without this step, every imported contact fires the welcome workflows.

STEP 4: Contacts → Import → map fields. FUB "Stage" field → map to a tag ("fub-stage:[value]"), NOT directly to the GHL pipeline stage.

STEP 5: Post-import: review 20-30 contact sample. Merge duplicates. Opt-out contacts: add tag "status:dnd" and activate the Opt-Out field in GHL.

STEP 6: Reactivate workflows. They'll now only fire on NEW contacts coming in through configured triggers.

Internal Tech Support
›An agent says a lead didn't receive the welcome SMS. How do I diagnose this?

Most frequent support case. 6 possible causes, check in this order:

1. Did the workflow even fire? Go to contact → Automations tab. Does the welcome workflow appear? If NOT: the trigger didn't activate. The lead was probably created in a way that didn't trigger it (manual import).

2. Did the workflow reach the SMS step? Click on workflow → execution log → identify where it stopped. "Skipped" = If/Else condition not met, not an error. "Failed" = continue to step 3.

3. Is the phone number valid for SMS? Verify: has +1, not a landline, no unusual characters.

4. Is the contact marked Opt-Out? "SMS Opt-Out" or "Do Not Contact" field active → GHL blocks all SMS without a visible error.

5. Is the A2P number still active? Settings → Phone Numbers → Status "Active" and A2P Compliance "Approved". Any flag blocks SMS for everyone.

6. Did it arrive but get carrier-filtered? Some carriers filter 10DLC numbers as spam. Not verifiable from GHL. Instruct agent: try contact via email or direct call.

›A workflow is running on contacts it shouldn't be. How do I stop it without breaking the ones that should continue?

Emergency situation. Act in this exact order:

STEP 1 (30 seconds): Automations → toggle to pause the workflow. Stops new entries but does NOT stop those already inside.

STEP 2: Remove affected contacts. If few: contact by contact → Automations → Remove from Workflow. If many: Contacts → filter by active workflow → select all → Bulk Action → Remove from Workflow.

STEP 3: Assess the damage. Were incorrect messages already sent? Execution log → "Completed Actions." If messages already went out: inform Jaime and Deb immediately. May require manual response to affected contacts.

STEP 4: Identify root cause. Most common error: trigger too broad. Did it have a creation date filter? A condition that the lead is new?

STEP 5: Fix the missing conditions. Create a test contact. Verify the workflow runs correctly. Reactivate.

›The chatbot isn't responding on Facebook/Instagram. What do I check first?

Known and documented issue. Checklist in order:

1. Settings → Integrations → Facebook → connection status. Did the token expire? Facebook tokens expire periodically. If expired: reconnect.

2. Facebook Business Suite → Settings → Connected Apps → GHL → verify "Manage Messages" permission. Without this GHL can receive but not reply.

3. Settings → Conversations AI → Driscoll-Peca Team bot → Channels tab. Are Facebook Messenger and Instagram ON? They sometimes self-deactivate after a Facebook reconnection.

4. Check that Facebook doesn't have "Instant Replies" active on the page. It can intercept messages before they reach GHL.

5. Test: send message from a personal Facebook account → wait 30 seconds. If it arrived in GHL but the bot didn't respond: problem in bot configuration. If it didn't arrive in GHL: problem in Facebook connection.

6. If everything checks out and it still doesn't work: create ticket with full evidence (screenshots of Conversations, Settings → Facebook, date/time of test).

›How do I read a workflow's Execution Log?

From the contact: Automations tab → click on the workflow → "View History." You see exactly which steps ran on that specific contact with timestamps.

From the workflow: Automations → click on workflow → "Enrollment History." You see all contacts that have passed through that workflow.

Status interpretation:

✅ COMPLETED: step executed successfully.

⏭ SKIPPED: If/Else condition not met. NOT an error. The workflow took another path.

❌ FAILED: tried to execute but couldn't. Causes: invalid number, bounced email, webhook failed, referenced field deleted.

⏸ PAUSED: waiting on a Wait step. Completely normal.

🚫 CANCELLED: stopped manually or by Exit Condition.

When reporting to GHL Support always include: exact workflow name, Contact ID (in the contact's URL), timestamp of the failed step, exact error message from the log.
